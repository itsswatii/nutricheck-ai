Sodium nitrate is a preservative commonly used in processed meats. It prevents bacterial growth and helps retain the red color of meat, but excessive consumption has been linked to health risks such as cancer.

Nutmeg is a spice made from the seeds of Myristica fragrans. It has a warm, nutty flavor and is used in baking and savory cooking. In large quantities, it can be toxic.

Hydrogenated oils contain trans fats, which have been shown to increase LDL cholesterol levels and contribute to heart disease.

Lactic acid is a naturally occurring organic acid used to add acidity to foods and preserve them. It is generally recognized as safe.

Isomalt is a sugar substitute made from beet sugar, often used in sugar-free candies. It has minimal impact on blood sugar but may cause bloating or gas in large quantities.
